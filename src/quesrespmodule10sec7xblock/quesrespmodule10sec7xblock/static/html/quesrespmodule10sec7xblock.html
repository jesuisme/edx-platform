TRUE
FALSE

Question 6 of 6

Incorrect.

Special cause variation only indicates that there is something significantly different about the process you are measuring. It does not always correlate with improvement or worsening of your measure nor indicate that the specific change you made is the cause of the variation. It is simply a sign that something has changed and is worth investigating.

Correct!

Special cause variation is a sign that something has changed in the process you are measuring that is worth investigating, but it doesn’t necessarily indicate that what you’ve done is causing that change. For example, perhaps you notice that the special cause variation correlates with an intervention you made to improve the process.
